# US-Brewery-Market-Analysis
**Finding the Best Location to Open a New Brewery**
This project conducts a data-driven analysis of U.S. brewery market to identify the most attractive states for opening a new brewery. Using brewery location data, census socioeconomic indicators, and rent data, we evaluate market opportunities based on **demand, competition, and operating costs**.

The analysis combines Python data analysis, SQL feature engineering, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and regression modeling to generate a ranked list of promising expansion markets.

## Project Goal
Identify U.S. states with:
- High market demand
- Strong purchasing power
- Low brewery competition
- Reasonable operating costs

The goal is to provide a data-driven framework for brewery location selection.

## Data Sources
- **Open Brewery DB API** — brewery locations across the U.S.
- **U.S. Census Bureau API** — population and household income
- **Zillow Observed Rent Index (ZORI)** — state-level average rent

## Methods
The analysis combines several techniques:
- **SQL analysis** — joins, subqueries, group by, window functions
- **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** — regional market patterns
- **Regression modeling** — identifying key drivers of brewery density
- **Composite scoring** — ranking states by market attractiveness

## Key Findings
- **Top Recommended States** — **Utah, Texas, and Oklahoma** combine strong demand, high purchasing power, and manageable entry costs.
- Population and income are the strongest drivers of brewery market potential.
- Several states show high income but relatively low brewery competition.
- Regional patterns reveal lower competition in the Southwest and Southeast.
